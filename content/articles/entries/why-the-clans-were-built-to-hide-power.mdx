The oldest mistake in this world is believing the clans were made to rule.

They were not.

They were made to hold power in separate hands so no single bloodline could shape the world by instinct alone. What looked like tradition was actually architecture. What looked like legacy was discipline built into lineage.

Each clan carried only one face of the divine. One carried destruction. Another carried chaos. Another balance. Another strategy. This division was not a celebration of difference. It was a defense against totality.

Because complete power does not negotiate with society. It does not ask permission from kings, priests, or empires. It bends the age around itself.

That is why the clans survived among humans instead of above them. They learned to disappear inside families, rituals, and inherited duty. They made secrecy look like culture. They made restraint look like virtue.

And for generations, that restraint held.

Until bloodlines emerged that were never meant to fit inside the design.

The moment power begins to combine what was meant to stay divided, the clan system stops being a structure of order and becomes a cage under pressure. That is where every great fracture begins.
