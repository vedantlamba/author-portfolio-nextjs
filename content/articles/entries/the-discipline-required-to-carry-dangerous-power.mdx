Power becomes dangerous long before it becomes visible.

It becomes dangerous the moment the person carrying it stops respecting the cost of holding it well.

In worlds built on divine inheritance, strength is never just a weapon. It is temperament. It is appetite. It is impulse given consequence. The stronger the bloodline, the less room there is for emotional carelessness.

That is why discipline matters more than talent.

A disciplined warrior can survive a flawed system. An undisciplined heir can destroy a perfect one. This is especially true for lineages born too close to chaos, rage, fear, or ungoverned force. Their first battle is rarely against an enemy. It is against overflow.

Dharma, then, is not a decorative virtue. It is structure placed inside the self.

It is the ability to pause before instinct becomes action. It is the ability to feel power rise without obeying it immediately. It is the quiet strength to remain dangerous without needing to prove it.

The bloodlines that endure are not always the strongest.

They are the ones that learn how to survive their own magnitude.
